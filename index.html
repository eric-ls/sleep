Sleep in America

The average amount of sleep Americans get every night has been on the decrease, and about 1 in 3 Americans don't get the recommended amount of sleep each night. Here's how you compare with the general population.

Immune System

Sleep deprivation also has a toll on your immune system. In a study, participants were given influenza vaccinations and their antibody response was measured over the course of a couple of weeks. Those who slept 8 hours showed much higher antibody response and retention.

Weight Gain

Compared to those who receive 8 hours of sleep, sleep-deprived individuals have a greater urge to eat, and their hunger sensation is amplified. Sleep-deprived individuals also have a larger appetite for carbohydrate-heavy foods.

General Attentiveness

Sleep deprived individuals also are less attentive. A test called the psychomotor vigilance test measures how "attentive" people are. Here's how you compare with someone who receives no sleep. Take note that the effects of sleep deprivation will stack up, so a person who sleeps 6 hours every night will perform just as worse as someone who pulls an all-nighter after 10 nights!

Memory

Students take note! Your memory also suffers if you don't sleep. In a recent study, individuals were asked to memorize syllables from a list. Those who slept after the initial task were able to recall much more than those who remained awake.

Tips for better sleep

Stick to a schedule

Go to bed at the same time every day, since it will strenghten your body's circadian rhythm. That will make falling and staying asleep easier.

Avoid naps

Naps during the day, especially in the afternoon, can also affect and reset your circadian rhythm. Try to limit naps to 10-30 minutes.

Be careful of what you eat

Try not to eat too much before bed, and watch the caffeine! Caffeine can take hours to wear off, so avoid it in the evening.

Make sure you sleep for 8 or more hours every night!
